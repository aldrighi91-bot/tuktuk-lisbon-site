Sintra

A fairy-tale village in the hills — colourful palaces, ancient Moorish castles and lush forest parks just 40 minutes from Lisbon. A UNESCO World Heritage Cultural Landscape.

2 / 8
Cascais

A charming coastal town where Lisbon's aristocracy once summered — beautiful beaches, a historic citadel and the wild Atlantic coastline stretching west.

3 / 8
Nazaré

Home to the world's biggest waves — surfers from across the globe come to ride the legendary swells of Praia do Norte. A traditional fishing village with a dramatic clifftop view.

4 / 8
Óbidos

A perfectly preserved medieval walled village — cobblestone streets, whitewashed houses trimmed in yellow and blue, and the famous ginja cherry liqueur served in chocolate cups.

5 / 8
Fátima

One of the most visited Catholic pilgrimage sites in the world — the Sanctuary of Fátima draws millions each year to the site of the 1917 Marian apparitions.

6 / 8
Évora

A UNESCO World Heritage city in the Alentejo — Roman temples, a medieval cathedral and the haunting Chapel of Bones built from the skulls of 5,000 monks.

7 / 8
Cabo da Roca

The westernmost point of continental Europe — where the land ends and the Atlantic begins. Standing here, the next stop west is America.
